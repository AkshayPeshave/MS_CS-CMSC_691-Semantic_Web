CMSC 691 HW3 - RDF and N3 using GEDCOM genealogy ontology specification

On this assignment I started off by reviewing:
  • The GEDCOM ontology and all property and event tags therein.
  • The inference logic specified in the GEDCOM family rules N3 file.

Following the sequence of the homework requirements, I created a N3 file containing my family details. I specified genealogy information for 5 nuclear families. I then interconnected these using :
  • Relationships like sibling
  • Having one person as a husband/father in his own nuclear family and as a child in his father's family
I also used the foaf vocabulary to assert, for the various people in my family, their:
  • Title (foaf:title)
  • Nicknames (foaf:nick)

Having tested the family details file to be error-free, I proceeded to create a family rules inference logic N3 specification of my own. As per requirements I first specified inference rules for :
  • A person knows her parents.
  • A person knows her children.
  • A person knows her siblings
Further, I specified rules for:
  • If a person A knows another person B, then person A would know person B by their nickname too.
  • Infering Sister-in-law and Brother-in-law GEDCOM relationships based on existing and/or inferred spouse and sibling relationships.
  • Propogating nephew and niece relationships amongst spouses.
  • Propogating citizenship GEDCOM values :
    • Amongst spouses (Citizenship status propogation by marriage).
    • From parents to their children (Citizenship status propogation through parents).


The facts about my family, both pre-specified and inferred, are stored in N3 format in a separate file for reference.
